# PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS DOCUMENT (PRD)

# Personalized Financial Literacy Platform (Working Name: Wilbur)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 1. PRODUCT OVERVIEW

## 1.1 Vision

Build the most approachable, personalized financial education platform
for everyday people --- especially first-gen professionals, immigrants,
liberal arts grads, and non-finance backgrounds --- using AI +
interactive visual learning.

**Core Promise:**\
"Finance explained for your life, not for Wall Street."

This product should feel:

-   Educational platform first\
-   AI tutor second\
-   Never an advisor

This product is not financial advice.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. PROBLEM

Many people are financially illiterate, and although there are many
financial articles and resources online, they are:

-   Extremely wordy\
-   Complicated\
-   Hard to understand\
-   Text-heavy\
-   Intimidating

Examples: - Investopedia\
- Nerdwallet\
- The Balance\
- Khan Academy

When people first start learning how to "adult" and manage finances, it
is overwhelming. There are many terms and systems to understand. But not
all of those things are relevant to every person at every stage of life.

Finance often feels: - Exclusive\
- Elite\
- Designed for those already educated in economics\
- Not built for first-generation learners

This product solves: Overwhelming, generic, jargon-heavy financial
education by tailoring learning to life stage, employment type, goals,
and confidence level.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. PERSONAL STORY (WHY THIS EXISTS)

My parents have never invested in stocks or gotten loans or a mortgage.
They were immigrants and did not understand the American financial
system. They never had corporate jobs, so they never had W2 income or
corporate retirement plans.

Coming out of college, when I started making my first paychecks, I
wanted to learn how to invest. But when I started researching:

-   Articles were extremely wordy\
-   YouTube videos were long\
-   Finance felt intimidating\
-   It took months to understand basics

Later, I realized many coworkers and friends had similar struggles:

-   A friend had never owned a credit card\
-   A graphic designer never had a 401k until switching jobs\
-   Many liberal arts students had zero finance education\
-   Many first-gen professionals had no one to ask

These are "adulting" skills that are not taught in high school or
college but have massive real-world impact.

Financial literacy should not be accessible only to elites.

Democratizing finance can lift people out of poverty and help build
generational wealth.

This product is about:

Confidence-building infrastructure for first-gen wealth creation.
